# 1998–2011 Ford Ranger V8 Swap Donor Guide

This reference outlines the four primary V8 engine families commonly swapped into the 1998–2011 generation Ford Ranger chassis, including their exact donor vehicles and transmission compatibility options.

---

## 1. Ford Small-Block Pushrod V8 (5.0L / 302ci)
This is the most common and well-documented swap for this generation of Ranger. Its compact pushrod architecture fits the engine bay with minimal structural modification to the firewall or steering components.

### Engine Donors
*   **1996–2001 Ford Explorer (5.0L V8)** 
*   **1997–2001 Mercury Mountaineer (5.0L V8)**
    *   *Note:* These are highly preferred. They feature the shortest front accessory drive (pulleys/water pump) to clear the Ranger's radiator core support, and high-flowing intake manifolds. 1996 to early 1997 models feature **GT40** heads (external EGR); late 1997 to 2001 models feature **GT40P** heads (internal EGR, requires specific swap headers to clear the spark plug angles).
*   **1987–1995 Ford Mustang (5.0L H.O.)**
    *   *Note:* Primarily used for 2WD builds. The front accessory dress from a Foxbody is longer and typically requires converting to the shorter Explorer timing cover and water pump assembly to prevent radiator interference.

### Transmission Compatibility
*   **Automatic:** **4R70W** (4-speed) sourced from a **1996–2001 Explorer/Mountaineer V8**.
    *   *4x4 Configuration:* The Explorer automatic is full-time AWD. To retain selectable 4x4 (High/Low ranges), builders swap the transfer case for a manual-shift **BorgWarner 4406** from a **1997–2003 Ford F-150**.
*   **Manual (4x4 or RWD):** **Mazda M5OD-R2** (5-speed) from a **1988–1996 Ford F-150** or full-size Bronco equipped with a **5.0L V8 or 4.9L Inline-6**. It must match this specific small-block bellhousing pattern.
*   **Manual (2WD Only):** **BorgWarner T-5** (5-speed) from a **1987–1995 Mustang V8**.

---

## 2. Ford Modular OHC V8s (4.6L / 5.4L / 6.8L V10)
These are wide, overhead-cam engines. They require significant fabrication, including modifying/notching the passenger-side heater box structure and creating a custom steering shaft layout with U-joints to clear the cylinder heads.

### Engine Donors
*   **1996–2010 Ford Mustang (4.6L V8 - 2-Valve or 3-Valve versions)**
*   **1992–2011 Ford Crown Victoria / Grand Marquis / Lincoln Town Car (4.6L 2V)**
*   **1997–2010 Ford F-150 / Ford Expedition (4.6L or 5.4L V8)**

### Transmission Compatibility
*   **Automatic:** **4R70W / 4R75W** or **4R100** sourced directly from the modular V8 donor car or truck to match the distinct modular bellhousing bolt pattern.
*   **Manual (2WD Only):** **Tremec T-45** or **TR-3650** 5-speed manuals from a **1996–2010 Mustang GT**.
*   **Manual (4x4):** **ZF 5-speed / 6-speed** from an F-250 Super Duty, or a modular-cased **Mazda M5OD-R2** from a **1997–2008 F-150 (4.2L V6 or 4.6L V8 applications)**.

---

## 3. Ford Generation 1 & 2 "Coyote" V8 (Modern DOHC 5.0L)
A modern, wide, high-horsepower dual overhead cam engine family. Like the older modular engines, it requires altering the passenger inner fender/heater core area and requires aftermarket standalone engine management control packs to run.

### Engine Donors
*   **2011–2017 Ford Mustang GT (5.0L V8)**
*   **2011–2017 Ford F-150 (5.0L V8)**
    *   *Note:* The truck variant features lower compression and unique camshaft profiles optimized for torque, but it is typically more abundant and less expensive in salvage yards.

### Transmission Compatibility
*   **Automatic:** **6R80** (6-speed automatic) sourced from a **2011–2017 Mustang or F-150**. For 4x4 capability, the F-150 variant and its matching transfer case must be used.
*   **Manual (2WD Only):** **Getrag MT82** (6-speed manual) from a **2011–2017 Mustang GT**.
*   **Manual (4x4):** Requires a specialized aftermarket bellhousing adapter plate to link the Coyote block to a truck manual transmission (such as a Tremec TKO or ZF6).

---

## 4. General Motors LS Platform (Pushrod V8)
Due to extensive aftermarket swap kit support, low cost, and a physically narrow pushrod footprint, GM LS configurations are highly popular alternatives.

### Engine Donors
*   **1999–2007 Chevy Silverado / GMC Sierra (4.8L, 5.3L, 6.0L "Vortec" truck engines)**
*   **1998–2002 Chevy Camaro / Pontiac Firebird (LS1 V8)**
*   **2005–2006 Pontiac GTO / Chevy Corvette (LS2 V8)**

### Transmission Compatibility
*   **Automatic:** **4L60E** or **4L80E** four-speed automatics pulled directly from the GM donor vehicle. For 4x4 retention, a GM truck-spec automatic is paired with an independent GM transfer case (such as the NP241 or NP246).
*   **Manual (2WD Only):** **Tremec T56** (6-speed manual) from a Camaro, Firebird, or GTO.
*   **Manual (4x4):** **New Venture NV3500** or **NV4500** 5-speed manuals sourced from a 4x4 Chevy Silverado / GMC Sierra.
